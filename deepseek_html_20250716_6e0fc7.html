Q: How do I solve quadratic equations using the quadratic formula?

A: The quadratic formula is x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)]/2a. First identify coefficients a, b, and c from your equation in the form ax²+bx+c=0. Plug these values into the formula, calculate the discriminant (b²-4ac), then solve for both roots using the + and - versions.

Q: What's the difference between mitosis and meiosis?

A: Mitosis produces two genetically identical diploid daughter cells for growth and repair, while meiosis produces four genetically unique haploid gametes (sex cells) for reproduction. Mitosis has one division, meiosis has two.
